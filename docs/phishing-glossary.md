# Phishing Glossary

## Purpose
This glossary explains common phishing-related terms used in this project.

## Phishing
A fraudulent attempt to trick a user into revealing sensitive information, opening a malicious file, or taking an unsafe action.

## Spear Phishing
A targeted phishing attempt aimed at a specific person, role, or organization.

## Business Email Compromise (BEC)
A phishing or fraud scenario where the attacker impersonates a trusted business contact, often to request payments, sensitive information, or urgent action.

## Social Engineering
Psychological manipulation used to influence a person into taking an action that benefits the attacker.

## Impersonation
Pretending to be a trusted individual, company, or service in order to gain credibility.

## Lookalike Domain
A domain name that visually resembles a legitimate one, often with small spelling changes or character substitutions.

## Reply-To Mismatch
A situation where the reply-to address differs from the visible sender address, which can indicate deception.

## Credential Harvesting
A phishing technique intended to steal usernames, passwords, or other login information.

## Attachment Lure
A phishing technique that uses a document or file as bait to get the target to open something unsafe.

## Double Extension
A misleading file naming trick such as `invoice.pdf.html` used to disguise the true file type.

## Urgency
Language designed to pressure the recipient into acting quickly without verifying the message.

## Secrecy
A tactic that tells the target not to discuss or verify the request, often used in BEC scams.

## Link Mismatch
A case where the visible text of a hyperlink appears legitimate, but the real destination is different.

## SPF
Sender Policy Framework. An email authentication mechanism that helps identify whether a sender is authorized to send mail for a domain.

## DKIM
DomainKeys Identified Mail. An email authentication mechanism that uses cryptographic signing to help verify message legitimacy.

## DMARC
Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance. A policy and reporting mechanism built on SPF and DKIM.

## Red Flag
An indicator that suggests an email may be suspicious or malicious.

## Sanitized Sample
A sample that has been cleaned of sensitive, private, or potentially harmful data so it can be safely shared in public.

## Verdict
The final analyst assessment of a sample, such as:
- Likely Phishing
- Suspicious
- Likely Benign
